The Effect Plastic Waste is Having on The Environment

Although plastics are already a common component of our daily lives, they also contribute a significant threat to the environment. The increased use of plastics has resulted in rising plastic trash, which is wreaking havoc on ecosystems, species, and the Earth as a whole.

Plastic contamination in the oceans is one of the main issues. Millions of tons of plastic waste are dumped into the oceans every year, where it may take them hundreds of years to break down. Ingestion, entanglement, habitat damage, and other effects of this plastic garbage can be harmful to marine life. For instance, when marine turtles mistake plastic bags for jellyfish, which are their natural meal, they ingest them and become severely bloated.

In addition to ocean pollution, plastics can cause land pollution and soil contamination in addition to polluting the ocean. Plastics have the potential to release dangerous substances into the groundwater and soil, endangering both human health and animals. According to United Nations Environment Programme From 1950-2017, approximately 76% of the plastic produced ended up as plastic waste in either landfills or was dumped elsewhere.

The only good and sustainable method of plastic disposal is recycling and even then, it only sits at 6%.

What is Being Done to Stop It

Ever since the issue has been raised that the world's plastic consumption and production rate has been exponentially increasing, companies have took initiative to eliminate the use of single-use plastics. Items such as plastic straws or plastic utensils, and instead are opting towards thing more eco-friendly such as biodegradable straws, or wooden utensils.

If you have been to a McDonald's within the past couple of years, you may have realized that they've steered away from their use of plastic straws and switched to the paper alternative to help conserve plastic waste. They've also cut out their plastic utensils and swapped to the wooden versions. As being the leading fast-food chain worldwide, McDonald's does a great job of setting the example of how to limit the use of plastics. Many organizations should take this approach as it is more eco-friendly and provides a more sustainable Earth.

McDonald's wasn't the first company to do this do. Limiting plastic consumption has always been an issue that's on everyone's agenda. But having a big company like McDonald's come out and make changes to better the environment only pushes other companies to do the same. Here you will find a list of large organizations who limit plastic use.

Environmentalist and ecologists are really pushing the narrative the stop the consumption of single-use plastics providing timelines of how long these take to break down. For example, a plastic bottle takes 500 years to become biodegradable; a plastic bag, 400 years and so on. There are companies that are coming out with new Technologies and solutions to help get rid of the enormous amount of plastic that's being trashed every single day. There has been innovations such as the Cat-HTR and plastic-eating bacterias to help limit the rate at which plastic is being inefficiently disposed.

Innovative Technologies to Help Eliminate Plastics

Cat-HTR

With there being an influx of plastic waste that seems to be exponentially growing, there are companies that strive to come up with innovative technologies to help reduce the amount of plastic the world consumes and produces. One company in particular has come up with the invention to help turn plastic waste into high-quality sustainable oil. A Biotechnology company in North Sydney, Australia named "Licella", has came up with an invention called the Cat-HTR (Catalytic Hydrothermal Reactor). This machine uses high-temperature and high pressure water to chemically changes plastic to return it to the oil it originally came from. The water acts like molecular scissors and is able to penetrate through the harmful chemicals in the plastic to get it back to its original state.

Plastic-Eating Bacteria

In early 2016, scientists in Japan were shocked to have discovered what seemed to be a bacteria with the ability to decompose , or "consume" plastic. The team of scientists were able to identify the bacteria as "Ideonella sakaiensis". At the time, this discovery was ready to change how the world handled plastic waste. However, after further research, the scientists realized that the plastic-eating bacteria was only able to decompose a particular type of plastic called 'PET'. On the other hand, most bottles are commonly made from this which was a good thing. But, it wouldn't be able to degrade the plastic quickly enough to eliminate the millions of pounds of plastic that's already here.

With this new discovery there has been a stronger desire to find or produce bacteria that are capable of breaking down and eating plastics on a larger scale. Scientists are constantly trying to find a solution for the overflow of plastic waste that continues to increase daily and plastic-eating bacteria might soon be the solution to this problem.
